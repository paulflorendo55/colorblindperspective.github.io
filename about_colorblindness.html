What is Colorblindness?

Color Vision Deficiency (CVD) is one of the most common disorders of vision and different studies show that it affects 8 to 10 % of the male general population and 0.5% of the female population worldwide (5). Most cases of CVD are genetic diseases (4) which mean that it can be inherited from parents to their children. It is an X-link recessive disorder (4) which means that it is more common in boys than in girls. A person can have CVD through congenital (inherited) or acquired. Congenital or inherited CVD is not due to certain disease, incurable, and is present throughout life. While for acquired color deficiency, it is usually a result of degenerative diseases, toxic effects of drugs, trauma, or aging (6).
